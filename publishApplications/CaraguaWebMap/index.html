URBIS-Caraguá

This application presents the results of the URBIS-Caraguá model, developed under the project 'Urbanization and Vulnerability: Computational Simulation Models as Boundary Objects', funded by the Brazilian National Council for Scientific and Technological Development (CNPq - Process 443052/2014-0). The URBIS-Caraguá model was developed by the Federal University of ABC (UFABC) and the Earth System Science Center of the National Institute for Space Research (CCST/INPE). The details of the model are described here.

URBIS-Caraguá

Feitosa et. al (2014)

Social Classes 2010



This is the main endogenous variable of the model. It was obtained from a classification that categorizes the social conditions of households in Caraguatatuba on "condition A" (best), "B" or "C". This classification was carried out through satellite imagery interpretation and a cluster analysis (k-means method) on a set of indicators build from census data of income, education, dependency ratio, householder gender, and occupation condition of households.

Occupational Classes (IBGE, 2010)



The occupational class describes the percentage of houses and apartments inside such areas that have occasional use. The dwelling is typically used in summer vacations and holidays.

Social Classes 2025



The base scenario considers the zoning proposed by the new master plan of Caraguatatuba. This scenario shows how the new master plan consolidates existing patterns and trends, not being able to force significant changes in relation to the risk distribution observed in 2010.
